Computes prices ("clean" or "dirty") for a coupon bond. The bond pays coupons \(q\delta t\) at times \((t_i)_{i=1,\dots,M}\) such that

\[ t_0 < t_1 \leq t_0+\delta t, \quad t_{i+1}-t_i= \delta t, \quad t_M = T. \]

The bond also pays notional \(N=1\) at maturity \(T\). The dirty or cash price of the bond is given by

\[ P^{d}(t_0) = q\delta t \sum_{i=1}^M e^{-\gamma(t_i-t_0)} + e^{-\gamma(T-t_0)}. \]

The clean price has the form:

\[ P^{c}(t_0) = P^d(t_0) - A(t_0) = P^d(t_0) - q(t_0-s), \]

where \(A(t_0)\) is the accrued interest and \(s\) is the previous coupon time.

Parameters
dRate\(q\) The coupon rate.
dPeriod\(\delta t\) The time interval between payments.
dMaturity\(T\) The maturity.
dYTM\(\gamma\) The continuously compounded yield to maturity.
dInitialTime\(t_0\) The initial time.
bCleanThis parameter controls the output. If its value is true, then we compute "clean" prices. Otherwise, we compute "dirty" prices that include the accrued interest from the next coupon.
Returns
The price ("clean" or "dirty") for the coupon bond.

Computes the (dollar) duration of a coupon bond. The bond pays coupons \(q\delta t\) at times \((t_i)_{i=1,\dots,M}\) such that

\[ t_0 < t_1 \leq t_0+\delta t, \quad t_{i+1}-t_i= \delta t, \quad t_M = T. \]

The bond also pays notional \(N=1\) at maturity \(T\). The duration for the bond at \(t_0\) defined as

\[ D = - P'(\gamma) = - \frac{dP}{d\gamma}, \]

where \(P = P(\gamma)\) is the `‘dirty’' price of the bond for YTM \(\gamma\).

Parameters

dPeriod( \(\delta t\)) The time interval between payments.
dMaturity( \(T\)) The maturity.
dYTM( \(\gamma\)) The continuously compounded yield to maturity.
dInitialTime( \(t_0\)) The initial time.
Returns
The price ("clean" or "dirty") for the coupon bond.

Computes the curve of forward yields to maturity for given cash flow. For \(t\in [t_0,t_M)\), the forward yield to maturity \(\gamma(t)\) solves the equation:

\[ F(t) = \sum_{t_i>t} P_ie^{-\gamma(t)(t_i-t)}, \]

where \(F(t)\) is the forward price computed at \(t_0\) for delivery at \(t\). In the forward contract, the buyer pays forward price \(F(t)\) at delivery time \(t\) and then receives payments \((P_i)\) at payments times \(t_i>t\).

Parameters

rForward\((F(t))\) The forward price curve.
dInitialTime\(t_0\) The initial time.
dY0The lower bound for the YTM.
dY1The upper bound for the YTM.
rRootThe root bracketing algorithm.
Returns
The forward price curve of a cash flow.

Computes the continuously compounded YTM for a coupon bond. The coupon bond pays coupons \(q\delta t\) at times \((t_i)_{i=1,\dots,M}\) such that

\[ t_0<t_1\leq t_0+\delta t, \quad t_{i+1}-t_i = \delta t,\quad t_M = T. \]

Yield to maturity \(\gamma\) solves the equation:

\[ S(t_0) = q\delta t \sum_{i=1}^M e^{-\gamma(t_i-t_0)} + e^{-\gamma(T-t_0)}. \]

We find \(\gamma\) using Newton's gradient descent method. If \(\gamma_n\) is yield's estimate at step \(n\), then we stop iterations as soon as

\[ |\gamma_{n+1}-\gamma_n|<\epsilon. \]
